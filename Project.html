Business Issue

Our client is National Savings and Investment (via ATOS). NS&I is a company which offers government backed savings products. A majority of NS&I’s customers are aged 50 years or older, and the company is under patronised by young people. The company has identified this as a problem, and have approached us to devise and implement an IT based solution with the aim of attracting younger customers to NS&I’s savings products. The reason NS&I is pursuing this demographic is because young people provide an opportunity to the company: They are less likely to already have a savings account compared to the older generation. The company hopes to secure their business to create a new generation of loyal customers. The wider context of the project is that NS&I hopes to digitise in its entirety. The problem is fairly open-ended: NS&I has not specified what kind of solution they are expecting. Thus, it is up to us to invent, design, develop, and finally implement a proof-of-concept solution to the problem.

Interviews

We conducted 7 interviews after distributing the survey. The purpose of the interviews was to gain a more detailed understanding of our target demographic. Since we already had quantitative data from the survey, we decided to perform semi-structured interviews as we felt these would yield richer responses. Where the survey gave us an insight into the general banking habits of millennials, we hoped the interviews would shed light on the motivations and context behind those habits.
The conversational nature of the interviews encouraged respondents to suggest what they desired from a bank, and what they felt NS&I products were lacking.
From our interviews we were able to conclude that our respondents valued a convenient and easy to use banking service that is available at their fingertips. They wanted savings products that were low risk, with some return.

Solution Overview

NS&I Mobile App and Premium Bond Pooling

Our proposed solution is two-fold. One aspect is a new financial product: group owned premium bonds. The second aspect are the IT solutions: we will create an extension to NS&I's web interface to facilitate the new financial product. Additionally, we will develop a mobile app that will allow users to interact with all features of their NS&I account,including our new product of premium bond pooling, from a smartphone or tablet.

Rationale for Proposed Solution

Having conducted a survey of our target demographic (Survey) we learned that most of them had not heard of NS&I before. Moreover, many of them are students who have very little money to save. This, combined with the fact that many of them expressed interest in saving money in a pool with their friends or colleagues, inspired our group owned premium bonds solution. We believe that the social aspect of the product will encourage users from the younger generation to invest in premium bonds and advertise NS&I's products to their friends. Furthermore, we believe that this initial exposure to NS&I will make this user group more likely to continue to use NS&I's products and features in the future.

We believe that the mobile app is a necessity in the current market as all major retail banks have a mobile app. 40% of millennials say they interact with their smartphones more than other people. 60% of millennials interact with their bank once a week. Thus there is a strong case for a mobile app that would facilitate more frequent and productive interaction between millennials and NS&I. Hence we feel NS&I is at a severe disadvantage by not having a mobile app.

We also considered other solutions such as a savings simulator that would show people a projection of earnings on their savings in different NS&I products, in order to educate them about the benefits of saving with specific products. We did not choose this solution because it would difficult to market, and it might not have the impact that NS&I desires. Also it does not fulfil NS&I's goal of further digitisation.

Another solution we had considered was to create an API that would extend NS&Is functionality to third party developers. This would allow a bigger audience of developers to create innovative solutions that could attract millenials to the NS&I. We did not choose this solution due to its heavy reliance on NS&I's back-end IT systems. We were unsure how much access we would be granted to the back-end, considering it contains sensitive financial information.
