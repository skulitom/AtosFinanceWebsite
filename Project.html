Project Overview

NS&I Mobile App and Premium Bond Pooling

Our proposed solution is two-fold. One aspect is a new financial product: group owned premium bonds. The second aspect are the IT solutions: we will create an extension to NS&I's web interface to facilitate the new financial product. Additionally, we will develop a mobile app that will allow users to interact with all features of their NS&I account,including our new product of premium bond pooling, from a smartphone or tablet.

Rationale for Proposed Solution

Having conducted a survey of our target demographic (Survey) we learned that most of them had not heard of NS&I before. Moreover, many of them are students who have very little money to save. This, combined with the fact that many of them expressed interest in saving money in a pool with their friends or colleagues, inspired our group owned premium bond solution. We believe that the social aspect of the product will encourage users from the younger generation to invest in premium bonds and advertise NS&I's products to their friends. Furthermore, we believe that this initial exposure to NS&I will make this user group more likely to continue to use NS&I's products and features in the future.

We believe that the mobile app is a necessity in the current market. All major retail banks have a mobile app, and we believe this is a major disadvantage. 40% of millennials say they interact with their smartphones more than other people. 60% of millennials interact with their bank once a week. Thus there is a strong case for a mobile app that would facilitate more frequent and productive interaction between millennials and NS&I.

We had also considered other solutions such as a savings simulator that would be able show people first the benefits of savings and at the same time compare NS&Is products against others to educate people about what is in the market. Our reason for not picking this solution over what we picked is due to the difficult marketing nature of this product and lack of technological aspect to this solution as in addition to bringing in millennials NS&I also liked to digitalize their products in its entirety.

Another solution we had considered was also to create a new API extending NS&Is functionality to third party developers to allow a bigger audience to develop new creative solutions that would be better suited to millennials. Our reason for not going with this solution was due to its heavy reliance on NS&Is backend and also we were not sure on how much accessibility we would be granted due to the sensitive nature of the information. Our client was also not very comfortable in accepting this solution due to the impact on their backend even though he did mention that he liked the creativity in this idea.

Design Aspects

There are several design aspects that must be acknowledged in order to make our application as easy to use as possible.

  • Simplicity
  • Visibility
  • Consistency
  • Feedback
Lets first consider simplicity, if there is too much information given to the user at one time, the user may find the amount of information intimidating and will likely stop using the app in the near future. To avoid this problem when designing the app there should be as less text as possible, especially labels as everything should be made intuitive to the end user.

Visibility concerns how visible certain aspects of the application are. This may be the placement of certain buttons in obvious and easy to find places or this could also be the contrast between text and background.Text written in colors that are too similar to each other will be difficult for a regular user to read and may be impossible for a person that is colorblind. Applications that are difficult for the user to navigate because of irregular button placements would take them too long to understand and ultimately they may switch to using a different service.

The application should be consistent in order for the application to be both simple and visible. One aspect of consistency is the layout of the app a consistent layout allows a user to better assume the positions of elements of importance much better which leads to learning the app much quicker.

Feedback is important as it acknowledges actions made by the user. Actions that are not acknowledged would most likely lead to the user doing the same thing many times (i.e. misunderstandings between the user and the application) ultimately leading to a result that they were not expecting.
